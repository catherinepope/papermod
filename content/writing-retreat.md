👋 I'm looking forward to seeing you at the writing retreat on 8th July. Here's some guidance on how to make the most of the event.

### Preparation

Writing retreats are most productive when you plan what you're going to work on. Especially if it's a _realistic_ plan! You'll probably produce more words than you expect, but it's important not to set yourself a daunting target. Aim for a slight stretch, rather than a completely unprecedented output. That slight stretch keeps you moving, rather than thinking it's OK to keep perfecting that sentence. As we discussed in the workshops, writing and editing are separate activities. Trying to do both at once slows us down 😵‍💫

If a word target is unhelpful, choose a specific section you'll work on. Maybe a chunk of a chapter, or your response to an important paper. Depending on your focus, you could use the word budgeting technique from session 2, where you break down your piece and assign a word target to each slice. Or the OCAR structure to sketch out the elements: Opening, Challenge, Action, Resolution. This is you performing the role of project manager, providing a framework for the writer.

Make sure you have any important sources available for the session. Most of us can't be trusted to look up anything online without disappearing down a rabbit hole 🐇 If gaps _do_ emerge while you're writing, make a note to address them afterwards. This approach also foregrounds our own voice, rather than relying too much on secondary sources.

Oh, and bring some good snacks, too. It's difficult to write without carbohydrates.[^1]
