Your research has the potential to reach far beyond academic publications, but only if it's written for the people you want to reach.

Whether you want to inform policy, change practice, or simply share your work with a wider public, getting your research out of the academy requires a different kind of writing. In this practical workshop, you'll learn how to identify your audiences, choose the right channels, and adapt your writing so that it connects: clearly, accessibly, and with purpose.

You'll learn how to:

- Identify who might benefit from your research and what they actually need to know.
- Choose the most appropriate channels for your audiences, from blogs and newsletters to policy briefs and social media.
- Write accessibly for non-specialist readers without sacrificing rigour or credibility.
- Structure a research blog post that people will want to read and share.
- Build a sustainable approach to public engagement that fits around your other commitments.

By the end of the workshop, you'll have:

- A clearer picture of your audiences and the channels best suited to reaching them.
- A draft or outline of a blog post for you to develop further.
- A plan for your next steps.

👩🏽‍🎓 **Audience:** Researchers at all stages and in all disciplines.

⏰ **Duration:** 3 hours
