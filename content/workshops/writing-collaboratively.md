Co-authored outputs are a cornerstone of research — but the process of writing with others is rarely as straightforward as it should be.

If there's one thing harder than academic writing, it's writing with other academics ...

Whether you're contributing to a multi-author paper, co-writing a grant application, or producing a report with colleagues across institutions, collaborative writing brings its own set of challenges: unclear expectations, competing schedules, inconsistent voices, and the perennial problem of who gets credit for what.

In this practical workshop, you'll learn how to set up a collaborative writing project for success from the outset — and how to produce a coherent, well-structured piece of writing together, from first draft to final submission.

You'll learn how to:

- Establish clear agreements at the start of a project: authorship, contributions, roles, and deadlines.
- Use the CRediT taxonomy to record and communicate contributions fairly and transparently.
- Plan and structure a collaborative piece of writing before anyone starts drafting, including techniques such as the Delphi Method for reaching consensus on content and direction.
- Manage multiple contributors effectively, including version control, document sharing, and feedback processes.
- Maintain a consistent voice and quality across contributions from different writers, and know when to bring in an editor.

By the end of the workshop, you'll have:

- A practical framework for setting up and managing any collaborative writing project.
- Templates and strategies you can put to use straight away.
- A plan for your next steps.
