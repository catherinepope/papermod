Do you keep waiting for a free week to write, only to find it never arrives? Finding time to write is one of the biggest challenges researchers face. The good news is that time is rarely the real problem.

In this practical workshop, you'll discover how to build a sustainable writing habit that fits around your existing commitments — without working harder or longer.

Drawing on research into academic productivity and the psychology of writing, you'll explore why traditional approaches so often fail and what to do instead. You'll identify your most productive writing time, design a realistic writing schedule, and protect that time from competing demands.

You'll learn how to:

- Understand your own energy rhythms and creative attention span.
- Design a writing schedule that is realistic, sustainable, and built around your life.
- Distinguish between writing time and writing-related tasks — and make the most of both.
- Tackle procrastination by understanding what's really driving it.
- Set boundaries around your writing time without causing conflict.

By the end of the workshop, you'll have:

- A personalised writing schedule ready to put into practice.
- Strategies for protecting your writing time.
- A plan for your next steps.

👩🏽‍🎓 **Audience:** Researchers at all stages and in all disciplines.
