By the middle of your PhD, you’ve already done a lot of reading. The challenge isn't usually finding more sources, it's knowing how to read strategically, retain what matters, and make the transition from reading to writing with confidence.

This workshop offers a practical framework for doing exactly that. We'll begin with the distinction between divergent and convergent reading, and how shifting consciously between the two can transform the way you approach a reading session. Then we'll look at how to read with clear intentions, manage your focus across longer texts, and capture what you've read in a way that feeds directly into your writing.

Here’s what we'll cover:

- Divergent and convergent reading: a model for reading with purpose.
- Setting reading intentions that connect to your writing goals.
- Managing focus and attention across long or demanding texts.
- Capturing what you've read: a simple, sustainable workflow. 
- Knowing when you've read enough and are ready to write.

By the end of the session, you'll have:

- A clear framework for approaching different kinds of reading tasks. 
- Practical strategies you can apply to your next reading session.
- An action plan for creating your personal reading-writing workflow.

👩🏽‍🎓 **Audience:** Early/mid-stage PhD researchers in the Arts, Humanities, and Social Sciences.
